Sulfates (e.g., Sodium Lauryl Sulfate, Sodium Laureth Sulfate):

Why they're used: Sulfates are surfactants that help create a lather, which can make shampooing feel more effective.
Potential harm: Sulfates can be toxic to aquatic organisms. They can also contribute to water pollution by creating excess foam in rivers and lakes, which can harm aquatic life. They can also strip the scalp of natural oils, leading to dryness and irritation. They may also cause allergic reactions and eye irritation.
